🌟 About ETESA

ETESA (Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering Students Association) is the official student body of the ENTC department. It acts as a bridge between faculty and students, encouraging participation, leadership, and holistic development.

Formed with the vision to empower students beyond academics, ETESA provides a platform where students can explore their creativity, technical interests, and interpersonal skills. From organizing national-level events to cultural and technical workshops, ETESA creates unforgettable experiences that help students grow personally and professionally.

🎯 Why ETESA is Important:

  • Provides leadership and event management opportunities to students
  • Helps in developing soft skills like communication, teamwork, and time management
  • Fosters technical and cultural talent through various competitions and activities
  • Encourages networking with alumni, industry experts, and other institutions
  • Builds confidence and a sense of responsibility among students

🌱 What We Learn:

  • Taking initiative and handling responsibilities with confidence
  • Effective planning, coordination, and time management
  • Problem-solving and decision-making in real-time scenarios
  • Working collaboratively in a diverse team environment
  • Leadership with empathy and efficiency

ETESA is not just a committee—it's a journey of growth, learning, and lifelong memories. It brings together enthusiastic minds to lead, innovate, and make a lasting impact on our department and institute.

Committee Clubs

Speaker Club

The Speaker Club is a vibrant platform dedicated to enhancing communication, public speaking, and leadership skills among students. Our aim is to empower individuals to express their ideas confidently, think critically, and communicate effectively in any setting. Through regular sessions, workshops, debates, group discussions, and guest lectures, members gain practical experience in public speaking, interpersonal communication, and persuasive storytelling. The club also encourages participation in speech contests, panel discussions, and intercollegiate oratory events.

Eureka! & Jidnyasa Coordinator

Eureka! & Jidnyasa is a prestigious national-level event conducted under the ETESA banner, aimed at fostering technical excellence and innovation among engineering students across the country. Eureka! focuses on technical paper presentations, encouraging participants to research, analyze, and present engineering advancements in various domains. Jidnyasa showcases innovative project presentations, providing a platform for students to exhibit their practical problem-solving and engineering design skills.

As the coordinator, the role involves end-to-end management of both events — from planning schedules and guiding participants to coordinating with judges and ensuring smooth execution. The coordinator not only ensures academic integrity and quality but also helps promote creativity, confidence, and national-level exposure for all involved.

Event Coordinator

The Event Coordinator plays a vital role in the successful planning and execution of all events organized under ETESA. From brainstorming ideas and creating event plans to coordinating with faculty, vendors, and student teams — the Event Coordinator ensures every event runs smoothly and efficiently. This includes managing logistics, preparing schedules, arranging resources, and overseeing on-the-ground execution during events such as Eureka!, Jidnyasa, Freshers, Farewell, PTA Meetings, and more.

With strong organizational skills and a proactive approach, the Event Coordinator acts as a bridge between the core committee and the execution team, ensuring that all events align with ETESA’s mission of fostering technical, cultural, and academic growth. The position demands leadership, adaptability, and a passion for bringing ideas to life.

Technical Club

The Technical Club of ETESA fosters innovation, coding, and hands-on learning through workshops, competitions, and project exhibitions. It aims to enhance students' technical skills in electronics, programming, and emerging technologies while promoting a culture of research and development.

Sports Club

A sports club is an organization that brings people together who share a common interest in sports. It provides a structured environment for members to participate in recreational or competitive activities, develop skills, and foster social connections. These clubs can be amateur or professional, and may focus on a single sport or multiple sports.

Publicity Club

A publicity club in association with students focuses on promoting the club itself, its events, and its members. It utilizes various media to create awareness and engage the student body. Key activities include event promotion, content creation, and media outreach, fostering communication and organizational skills among students.

Cultural Club

The Cultural Club celebrates the rich diversity of art, music, dance, drama, and traditions. It provides a platform for students to express their creativity and showcase their talents through various cultural events, festivals, and competitions. With a focus on inclusivity and vibrancy, the club fosters unity and brings the campus alive with colors, rhythms, and joy.

Decoration Club

A decoration club in a student association focuses on enhancing spaces, events, and overall aesthetics. It provides a platform for students to develop skills in design, decoration, and creative problem-solving, often through hands-on activities and collaborative projects. These clubs contribute to a more vibrant and engaging campus environment.

Literature Club

Literary club is to make the students understand, analyze and gain knowledge through the process of reading. The primary objective of the literary club is to promote the literary talents of the students. Moreover, it provides them with the chance to express their creativity and talents in the best possible way.

Marathi-Sahitya Mandal

The Marathi Sahitya Mandal, also known as Marathi Vangmay Mandal, is a student association focused on promoting and preserving the Marathi language and culture. It provides a platform for students to showcase their talents in various art forms like singing, dancing, and poetry, and encourages discussions on social issues.

Treasure Hunt

Date: 3 April 2025

Participants:51 Teams (204 participants)

View Participants List

The Treasure Hunt event was one of the most thrilling and interactive events organized by the association, designed to challenge students’ logic, teamwork, and thinking under pressure.

The event consisted of four creative and adventurous rounds:

  • Round 1: Mobile Quiz Round – Participants answered a fun mix of movie-based riddles, logical questions, and basic math puzzles through an online mobile-based test. This round was both humorous and brain-teasing to set the tone for the hunt.
  • Round 2: Sudoku Round – Teams that qualified from Round 1 had to solve a time-based Sudoku puzzle. The goal was to test analytical thinking and pattern recognition skills under time pressure.
  • Round 3: Hunting Round – The real hunt began as participants searched across the college campus to find hidden cheats. Each team had to collect four cheats, where each clue led to the next. The progress depended entirely on solving the previous clue.
  • Round 4: Diamond Round – The final and most exciting round. Shortlisted teams received four final cheats containing hints to the exact location of a hidden “diamond.” The last cheat led to the ultimate destination. The first team to find it was declared the winner!

The event was filled with excitement, suspense, and laughter. It was an outstanding example of how fun learning, problem-solving, and teamwork can be when blended with creativity.

🏆 Winners:

  • 🥇 First Place: Shreyash Rokade & Team (S.Y. ETC)
  • 🥈 Second Place: Shweta Kale & Team (F.Y.)
  • 🥉 Third Place: Siddhi Ghadge & Team (F.Y. CSBS)

The farewell ceremony for our final-year B.Tech students was a heartfelt and emotional gathering, filled with warm memories, laughter, and gratitude. As the students bid adieu to their undergraduate journey, our department celebrated their achievements and contributions with great pride.
